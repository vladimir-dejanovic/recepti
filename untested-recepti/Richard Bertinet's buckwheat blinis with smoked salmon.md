Makes 8-10 blinis to serve for breakfast or as a starter OR 24 as canapés

## For the blinis

- 75g buckwheat flour
- 75g gluten-free flour
- A pinch of fine sea salt
- 80g crème fraîche, plus extra for topping (see below)
- 150g whole milk
- 10g fresh yeast, or 7g dried ‘quick’ or ‘fast action’ yeast
- 2 eggs
- Vegetable oil, for cooking


## To serve

- 8 slices of smoked salmon
- Crème fraîche
- Fresh dill, to garnish
- Fish roe, to garnish (optional)

## Method

Cut the smoked salmon into three strips per slice if making canapé-sized blinis and set aside. Mix the flours and salt together in a large bowl. Put the crème fraîche into a separate bowl. Gently warm the milk in a pan over a low heat.


While the milk warms, separate the eggs. Mix the yolks into the crème fraîche and keep the whites in another bowl for later.


When the milk is just below boiling point, remove it from the heat. Crumble in the fresh yeast or add the dried yeast and whisk to combine.


Whisk the warm milk into the crème fraîche and egg yolk mixture. Pour this into the flour and whisk well to make a thick batter. Cover with a cloth and leave in a warm place for about an hour, until risen, bubbly, and spongy.


Whisk the egg whites until they hold firm peaks. Gently fold them into the batter a little at a time, being careful not to overmix. Cover again and rest for a further 15 minutes.


Lightly oil your pan and heat over medium. Ladle in the batter (two large blinis or six small). When bubbles appear and the edges colour, flip and cook the other side for the same amount of time. Transfer to a wire rack and repeat.


To serve, fold the smoked salmon strips and place on top of each blini. Add a spoonful of crème fraîche, garnish with fresh dill, and finish with fish roe if you like.
