# Gingerbread Cookies - Prague

## Ingridients

- 700g-750g (all purpose) flour
- 300g honey
- 100g icing sugar
- 2 eggs
- 2 tablespoons of thick sour cream or natural yoghurt
- 90g-100g butter
- 1 flat teaspoon of baking soda
- 1-1.5 packet of gingerbread seasoning (20g)
- 2 teaspoons of cinnamon
- 2 tablespoons of cocoa
- 1 pinch of salt

## Method

Put honey and butter into a pan - just warm up (don't boil!) and add the gingerbread seasoning, cinnamon and cocoa. Stir the mixture and let it chill.
Sift the flour to a food processor's bowl, pour the chilled honey mixture and add baking soda, eggs and cream. Stir everything for about 4 minutes (use a dough hook and not a whisk!). 
Put the dough on a pastry board sprinkled with flour and knead till it is smooth and glossy. The dough should be dense and flexible, not to loose.
Wrap the dough in cling film and chill it in a fridge or freezer for about 60 minutes.
Cut the dough into small pieces and roll them on the sprinkled with flour pastry board using a common rolling ping to about 0.6mm (remember to sprinkle the dought surface as well). Next, roll the surface firmly with an engraved rolling pin. 
Cut out shapes with a cookie cutter and put the ginger breads on a baking tray covered with baking paper/mat. Put it inito a fridge for 15 minutes. This extra chilling helps to preserve a nice pattern.
Bake the cookies for about 10 minutes at 170 C.
